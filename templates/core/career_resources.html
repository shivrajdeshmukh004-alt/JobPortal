Resume Writing Tips

Keep it Concise

Limit your resume to 1-2 pages. Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds on initial resume reviews.

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "Led," "Developed," "Implemented," or "Achieved."

Quantify Achievements

Include numbers and metrics: "Increased sales by 35%" or "Managed team of 12 developers."

Tailor to the Job

Customize your resume for each application by highlighting relevant skills and experiences.

Optimize for ATS

Use standard section headings and include keywords from the job description.

Interview Preparation

Research the Company

Understand the company's mission, values, products, and recent news before your interview.

Practice Common Questions

Prepare answers for "Tell me about yourself," "Why this company?" and "What are your strengths?"

Use the STAR Method

Structure behavioral answers: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

Prepare Questions

Have 3-5 thoughtful questions ready to ask the interviewer about the role and company.

Follow Up

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours expressing your interest and appreciation.

Career Growth Strategies

Continuous Learning

Stay updated with industry trends and invest in professional development and certifications.

Build Your Network

Attend industry events, join professional groups, and maintain relationships with colleagues.

Seek Mentorship

Find mentors who can provide guidance and help you navigate your career path.

Take on Challenges

Volunteer for stretch assignments and projects outside your comfort zone.

Document Achievements

Keep a record of your accomplishments for performance reviews and future job applications.
